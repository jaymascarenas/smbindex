About --company name--
The --company name-- is a small business index that reflects the health of the US small business economy. Leveraging billions of credit and debit card transactions from small businesses that process with --company name--, the --company name-- provides the definitive analysis of consumer spending at small businesses across the United States. --company name-- defines a small business as: (1) a business that had total payment card sales volume across all stores of no more than $10 million in the previous 12 months and (2) a business that operated less than 10 locations during that period. National brands, merchants with little or no activity in the previous year, and merchants with outlying year-over-year sales growth or declines are excluded from the data.

Performance
The Performance Index reflects U.S. small business growth trends based on actual consumer spending. The index is a weighted measure of the difference between positive and negative sales growth, factored by the number of merchants with same-store year-over-year sales included in the data set.

Industry Growth
Growth by Sector showcases several industries based on same-store year-over-year analysis of key performance metrics including sales, transaction volume and average ticket size.

Regional Analysis
Growth by Region is a color-coded geospatial view of key performance metrics including same-store year-over-year sales growth, transaction volume and average ticket size.

Customer Spend
Spend per Card displays the same-store year-over-year sales growth trends in average spend per payment card for several industries.

Customer Acquisition
Customer Acquisition highlights the growth of new customers acquired on a same-store year-over-year sales analysis for several industries.
